Why STO conversion is flat, and what's working

19.25%
Avg STO conversion · target ≥ 30%

Conversion rate flat — main causes below. The gap to target is driven by a mix of client-side factors (lead quality, timing, budget) and transport/pricing constraints on larger and out-of-area jobs. Brand awareness is clearly building, which is improving lead quality over time.

Non-conversion overview — where the other ~80% goes

20%
Provided pricing — client selected an alternate storage option. Percentage is higher when and where a major storage provider has specials or a price war (i.e. currently in Gympie).
15%
Not contactable — no pricing provided due to lack of information/inventory.
10%
Requested pricing due to lease end notice, but landlord decided to extend the lease.
10%
Requested pricing due to lease end; did have to move but was able to move direct to a new rental — some of these may turn into removals jobs.
9%
Provided pricing — client advised too expensive. No clear picture of what solution, if any, the client eventually opted for; can include selling goods or stashing at a friend's place.
5%
Client has no immediate plan to move or store — just considering future options, e.g. downsizing in 12–24 months.
4%
House on market but has not sold, so the enquiry does not convert to a booking.
2%
Rents a shipping container instead (particularly areas with larger land sizes).
2%
Enquiry made by a friend or relative, not a direct client request — some of these do book, so the total category is possibly 5%.
2%
Out of area (these are notified to marketing).
1%
Client interested in booking, but too many red flags in terms of their ability to pay storage fees.
*Not empirical data — indicative estimates.

Positives

Brand

Brand awareness increasing

Clients are citing social media ads and seeing units around the area.

Lead quality

Better-informed callers

Some clients now call already knowing what they want — shortening the path to a quote.

Marketing loop

Out-of-area leads flagged

Out-of-area leads (Brisbane, Bribie Island) are flagged to Nick regularly so marketing can be adjusted or blocked accordingly.

Client-side issues

Lead quality

Unreachable or under-budget

Many enquiries are unreachable on follow-up, or their budget is too low to proceed.

Lead quality

Price-shoppers with no urgency

Lots of price-shoppers: renovating, garage full, "maybe" storage — no urgency or no clear need yet.

Timing

Quoting too early

Some haven't started house planning; others are quoting for next year — parked as lost until then.

Competition

Shipping containers instead

Some choose shipping containers — cheaper long-term for their situation.

Transport / pricing issues

Capacity

Large jobs need return trips

4-bedroom storage jobs need 5+ MSUs, but the truck maxes out at 5 per trip — return trips push fees too high.

Geography

Caloundra demand vs Cooroy base

Rising Caloundra enquiries (good for brand awareness), but Cooroy-based transport fees are too high for most.

In testing

Landsborough depot quotes

Testing quotes from the proposed Landsborough depot — too early to say if it's lifting conversion; will report once data's in.
